Faculty of Enginerring

The Faculty of Engineering will soon move to its new site at Akatakye; this new campu is laden with modern state-of-the-art facilities. Under the auspices of the Industrial Liaison Office of Takoradi Technical University, the Faculty has had an educational tour to the Bui Dam. Consequently, a three-year employment contract for products of the Faculty has been signed with the Bui Dam Authority.

  • The Faculty has a Petrochemical and Hydraulics Laboratory installed by Amatrol Inc., Jefferson, Indiana, USA. Consequently, short courses are organised for students and the public to enable them to acquire technological skills..
  • The Faculty has a modern Autotronic Laboratory at the Mechanical Engineering Department for the diagnosis of vehicle problems. This has expanded the training base of artisans. The Faculty intends to run short courses to upgrade the skills of artisans, both within and outside the University.

The Faculty of Engineering is the premier Faculty of the Takoradi Technical University. It began with Craft programmes in 1953. Currently, the Faculty has five (5) departments; the Faculty runs HND, B Tech and Technician programmes. The Faculty has a student population of over two thousand, three hundred (2,300) and a staff population of eighty-five (85). The Faculty is also equipped with state-of-the-art facilities for the training of students and the execution of various projects.

ACHIEVEMENTS
Many of the students of the Faculty of Engineering are working in reputable oil companies – Tullow Ghana Limited, Bart Long, Balkan Energy etc. Because of the excellent performances of our students, Tullow and Balkan have signed a special Memorandum of Understanding with the University for the establishment of a modern laboratory for control and instrumentation systems for the training of skilled technicians. Some of our staff are on exchange programmes at: the North Alberta Institute of Technology (Canada); and Amatol Inc. Indiamopolis (USA).

Faculty of apllied Art
The Faculty of Applied Arts and Technology is one of the five (5) faculties of the Takoradi Technical University. It started from the then Painting and Decoration Unit of the Building Department in 1952. The Fashion Department was also created; both grew and the Faculty was later christened, ‘School of Applied Arts’ in 2008. Its name has currently been changed to the ‘Faculty of Applied Arts and Technology’, in line with the conversion of the institution to the status of technical university. The Faculty is presently very vibrant and very resourceful because of the currency of its programmes and the availability of highly motivated staff who work assiduously to ensure that market-ready students are produced for the job market.

Goals and Objectives
The goals and objectives of the Faculty are to:

  • contribute to employment and human resource capacity building through competency-based tertiary education;
  • provide other related services such as research and consultancy to enhance productivity in industry; as well as offer Higher National Diploma (HND), degree and Master of Technology programmes in
  • selected areas required for middle-level and higher level employment capacity building.

Faculyt of business studies

The Faculty of Business Studies is the business wing of the University. It has five (5) Departments, namely: Accounting & Finance, Procurement & Supply, Professional Studies, Marketing & Strategy and Secretaryship & Management Studies. All the Departments offer programmes leading to the award of Higher National Diploma in Business Studies and Bachelor of Technology degrees in Business Studies. Among the Bachelor of Technology programmes offered are: Accounting with Computing, Marketing (all in collaboration with Kumasi Technical University), Secretaryship & Management Studies (in collaboration with Koforidua Technical University) and Procurement Management.

MISSION
To offer full-time tertiary programmes relevant to commerce and industry in order to supply middle and high-level manpower for the economic development of Western Region and Ghana as a whole.
